Industrial process automation?

Industrial automation has many benefits, one of which is lower operating costs. This is achieved by eliminating the need for healthcare, paid leave, and holiday costs for human operators. Additionally, industrial automation does not require employee benefits such as bonuses or pension coverage. Although there is a high initial cost for industrial automation, the long-term savings from not having to pay human workers' wages can be substantial. Additionally, the machinery used for industrial automation is less expensive to maintain as it does not often break down and if it does, it can be repaired by computer and maintenance engineers.

High Productivity

Industrial automation allows companies to run manufacturing plants 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, and 365 days a year, thus resulting in a significant improvement in productivity as compared to hiring hundreds of production workers for up to three shifts.

High Quality

Automation eliminates errors associated with human operators and produces products with uniform quality at all times as robots do not experience fatigue.

High Flexibility

Robots can be programmed to perform any task, making the manufacturing process more flexible as compared to training human operators for a new task on the assembly line.

High Information Accuracy

Automated data collection allows for the collection of key production information, improves data accuracy, and reduces data collection costs, providing the facts to make informed decisions on reducing waste and improving processes.

High Safety

Industrial automation improves the safety of the production line by deploying robots to handle hazardous conditions.
